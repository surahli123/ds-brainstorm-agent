# Audience Calibration (Numeric 1-10)

## What to evaluate

Is the technical depth appropriate for the stated or implied audience? Are jargon and acronyms explained when needed? Is the level of detail right?

## Scoring Anchors

- **9-10:** Perfectly calibrated; technical depth matches audience throughout
- **7-8:** Mostly well-calibrated; occasional jargon without explanation
- **5-6:** Uneven calibration; some sections too technical, others too shallow
- **3-4:** Significant mismatch between content and audience
- **1-2:** Written for the wrong audience entirely

## What moves the score up

- Acronyms and jargon defined on first use (e.g., "Designated Market Areas (DMAs)")
- Technical concepts explained with plain-language analogies
- Consistent depth throughout — not oscillating between dense and shallow
- Concrete examples illustrating abstract concepts

## What moves the score down

- Unexplained acronyms: DMA, BSTS, SC, HTE used without context
- Sections that assume specialized knowledge not established earlier
- Mixing audience levels: exec summary uses business language but methodology uses raw statistical jargon
