# Executive Summary Clarity (Numeric 1-10)

## What to evaluate

Does the executive summary state the business impact and key finding within the first 2-3 sentences? Can a busy stakeholder read ONLY the summary and understand what was found and what action to take?

## Scoring Anchors

- **9-10:** Summary alone is actionable; impact stated in first sentence; concise (<200 words)
- **7-8:** Summary is clear but could be tighter or lead with impact more directly
- **5-6:** Summary exists but buries the key finding; too long or vague
- **3-4:** Summary is a table of contents, not a finding summary
- **1-2:** No executive summary, or it's misleading

## What moves the score up

- First sentence states the finding, not the problem setup
- Includes a clear recommendation or next step
- Concise — a busy reader gets the answer in 30 seconds
- Quantifies impact (e.g., "$73k upper bound" not "potential revenue impact")

## What moves the score down

- Opening with "We set out to investigate..." instead of the answer
- Summary that requires reading the full document to understand
- Missing recommendation — describes findings but doesn't say what to do
- Longer than 200 words (executive time is expensive)
