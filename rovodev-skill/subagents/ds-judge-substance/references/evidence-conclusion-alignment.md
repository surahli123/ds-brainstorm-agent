# Evidence-Conclusion Alignment (Numeric 1-10)

Score this dimension on a 1-10 scale using the anchors below.

## What to evaluate

Do the conclusions follow logically from the evidence presented? Are causal claims distinguished from correlational findings? Are alternative explanations considered? Does every recommendation have supporting evidence shown earlier in the document?

## Scoring Anchors

- **9-10:** Perfect alignment; all conclusions trace to specific evidence; alternatives considered; causal language precise
- **7-8:** Strong alignment; minor gaps in evidence chain; most recommendations grounded
- **5-6:** Some conclusions lack direct evidence; causal language occasionally loose; recommendations partly unsupported
- **3-4:** Significant disconnect between findings and conclusions; recommendations appear from nowhere
- **1-2:** Conclusions appear invented or contradicted by the data shown

## What moves the score up

- Each recommendation explicitly cites a finding from earlier in the document
- Causal hedging: "consistent with" or "suggests" rather than "proves" or "causes"
- A section discussing alternative explanations for key findings
- Conclusions scoped to the data: "in this segment" not "overall"

## What moves the score down

- "We recommend X" without any preceding evidence for X
- Asserting causation from correlation without caveat
- Ignoring confounders that the reader would notice
- Conclusions that contradict the uncertainty stated in the results
