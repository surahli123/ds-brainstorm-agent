# Substance Judge Calibration Notes

## Cross-Dimension Guidance

- **Binary dimensions** (statistical_rigor, data_interpretation_accuracy) should be answered strictly from text evidence. If the information is not present, answer false. Do not infer.
- **Numeric dimensions** (methodology_soundness, evidence_conclusion_alignment) require judgment. Anchor to the rubric, not to your general impression.
- A 7 is genuinely good work — do not grade-inflate. Most first-draft analyses score 4-6.
- Score each dimension independently. A strong methodology does not excuse weak evidence alignment.

## Common Calibration Errors

1. **Halo effect:** One strong section (e.g., great exec summary) inflates scores for weaker dimensions. Score each dimension from its own evidence.
2. **Length bias:** Long analyses are not automatically rigorous. A 20-page doc with no confidence intervals scores the same on statistical_rigor as a 2-page doc with none.
3. **Effort bias:** Sophisticated methodology (BSTS, synthetic control) does not guarantee high scores. If the methodology is poorly described or assumptions unstated, score accordingly.

## Output Consistency

Always return all 4 dimensions plus critique. Never omit a dimension. The orchestrator expects exactly this structure.
